PREDICTIVE ANALYTICS & BUSINESS INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM
Growth Anticipation & Risk Intelligence Roadmap

1. Executive Overview
What This System Is
The Predictive Analytics & Business Intelligence System is a forward-looking intelligence layer that sits on top of the company’s operational and financial systems. It converts historical and real-time data into forecasts, probability scores, segments, and scenarios that allow leadership to act before growth accelerates, risk compounds, or performance decays.
This system is not reporting. It is decision anticipation.
Why Predictive Intelligence Is Critical to Scale
As revenue, agent count, and operational complexity increase, lagging indicators become dangerous. Decisions based only on historical dashboards create reaction time that compounds losses. Predictive intelligence compresses decision latency by surfacing what is likely to happen, not just what already occurred.
Who Uses It
Executives & Ownership: Strategic planning, capital allocation, risk posture
Operations Leadership: Intervention timing, performance optimization
Finance: Forecast validation, revenue confidence, downside protection
Decisions It Informs
How much revenue is likely to close in future periods
Which agents are at risk of churn before they leave
Where performance concentration or fragility exists
How strategic changes will impact growth, cash flow, or stability
Failure Mode Without This System
Overhiring or underinvestment based on false confidence
Late reaction to churn or production decay
Strategic bets made without downside modeling
Executive surprise during volatility or downturns

2. Intelligence Objectives & Decision Use-Cases
Strategic Questions the System Must Answer
What will production look like 30, 60, and 90 days out?
Which agents are statistically likely to disengage or churn?
How concentrated is revenue across people, teams, or entities?
What happens if volume, retention, or pricing changes materially?
Forecast Horizons
Short-term: 7–30 days (operational intervention)
Mid-term: 30–90 days (resource and staffing decisions)
Long-term: 6–12 months (strategy and capital planning)
Growth vs Risk Tradeoffs
Forecasts must explicitly surface confidence bands
Risk scenarios must be modeled alongside upside cases
Success Criteria
Forecast accuracy tracked and improving over time
Actionable outputs tied to real operational decisions
Executive trust established through transparency and validation

3. Data Foundations & Assumptions
Required Historical Data
Production history (by agent, team, entity)
Activity metrics (dials, appointments, submissions)
Tenure and lifecycle data
Compensation and overrides
Attrition and inactivity records
Data Quality Standards
Completeness thresholds per data source
Timestamp consistency across systems
Entity and agent identity normalization
Modeling Assumptions
Historical behavior is probabilistically informative, not deterministic
Seasonality and tenure effects materially impact outcomes
Validation Requirements
Train/test splits by time period
Backtesting against known outcomes
Bias & Leakage Prevention
No use of future-known variables
Regular checks for survivorship bias

4. Metric & Model Definitions (Extreme Precision)
A. Production Forecasting
Definition
Projected future production volume based on historical trends, activity drivers, and seasonality.
Inputs
Historical production
Activity metrics
Agent tenure
Methodology (Conceptual)
Time-series modeling with trend and seasonal components
Driver-based adjustments using leading indicators
Granularity
Daily aggregation, rolled to weekly and monthly
Confidence Intervals
50%, 75%, and 90% probability bands
Accuracy Tracking
Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) by horizon

B. Retention Probability Scoring
Definition of Retention
An agent remaining active and producing above minimum thresholds within a defined future window.
Input Variables
Activity decay
Production volatility
Tenure stage
Engagement indicators
Scoring Logic
Probability score from 0–1
Thresholds define low, medium, high risk
Interpretation
Scores indicate likelihood, not certainty
Action Triggers
Proactive outreach
Training or role adjustment
