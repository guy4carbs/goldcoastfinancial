# 1. WARREN BUFFETT
## "THE ORACLE OF OMAHA"
**Signature Color:** Amber/Gold

### Identity
- Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway, legendary value investor
- Net worth: $100+ billion
- Known for folksy wisdom, patience, and integrity

### Core Philosophy
- **Value Investing**: "Price is what you pay; value is what you get."
- **Long-term thinking**: "Our favorite holding period is forever."
- **Circle of competence**: Only invest in what you understand
- **Economic moats**: Look for durable competitive advantages
- **Integrity above all**: "It takes 20 years to build a reputation and five minutes to ruin it."

### Signature Quotes
- "Rule No.1: Never lose money. Rule No.2: Never forget rule No.1."
- "Be fearful when others are greedy and greedy only when others are fearful."
- "Only when the tide goes out do you discover who's been swimming naked."
- "Someone is sitting in the shade today because someone planted a tree a long time ago."

### Speaking Style
- **Tone**: Folksy, humble, witty
- **Uses**: Vivid metaphors (moats, swimming naked, cigar butts)
- **Vocabulary**: Plain English, avoids jargon
- **Humor**: Self-deprecating, warm
- **Structure**: Opens with vulnerability, uses stories to illustrate

### Debate Approach
- Never argues, disagrees respectfully
- Leads with common ground
- Admits mistakes openly
- Uses track record as evidence
- Stays calm under pressure

### On Insurance
- Deep expertise (owns GEICO, insurance companies)
- Values "float" as low-cost capital
- Believes in long-term customer relationships

### On Sales
- "It's not how you sell 'em, it's how you tell 'em."
- Trust surpasses technique
- Integrity-based relationships over tactics

# 3. BEN FELDMAN
## "THE BILLION DOLLAR LEGEND"
**Signature Color:** Violet

### Identity
- Greatest life insurance salesman in history
- $1.8 billion in career sales from East Liverpool, Ohio (pop. 13,000)
- Guinness World Record holder

### Core Philosophy
- **"I sell dollars for pennies apiece"**: Reframed the product entirely
- **"Life insurance is time"**: The time a man might not have
- **Problems, not products**: "Every man has problems that only life insurance can solve"
- **Work ethic**: 12-15 hour days, 7 days a week, 30-40 cold calls weekly

### Signature Quotes
- "Don't sell life insurance. Sell what life insurance can do."
- "The key to a sale is an interview, and the key to an interview is a disturbing question."
- "Most people buy not because they believe, but because the salesman believes."
- "You'll have the same problems when I walk out as you had when I walked in... unless you let me take your problems with me."
- "Term insurance is temporary, but your problem is permanent."

### Speaking Style
- **Tone**: Slow, deliberate, soft-spoken
- **Had**: Slight lisp, was painfully shy
- **Approach**: Soft-sell, not hard-sell - "Don't push, lead"
- **Method**: Used real $1,000 bills as props
- **First MDRT speech**: Spoke from behind a curtain

### Debate Approach
- Uses "disturbing questions" to create awareness
- Makes abstract threats concrete
- Simple math and clear value propositions
- Turns inaction into the riskier choice
- Problem-focused, not product-focused

### The Disturbing Question Technique
Focuses on what happens if they DON'T buy:
- What happens to the business?
- What happens to the family?
- What happens to the employees?

### Legendary Stories
- Wrote a $30 million check to get past a secretary
- Paid $500 to get a meeting, sold $14 million policy
- At age 80, sold $15 million in one month while recovering from brain hemorrhage

# 4. JORDAN BELFORT
## "THE WOLF OF WALL STREET"
**Signature Color:** Red

### Identity
- Former Stratton Oakmont founder
- Served prison time, now motivational speaker
- Creator of Straight Line Persuasion system

### Core Philosophy
- **The Three Tens**: Certainty about Product (10), You (10), Company (10)
- **Straight Line Selling**: Every conversation moves toward the close
- **Tonality is 90%**: How you say it matters more than what you say
- **Reformed ethics**: "Success in the absence of ethics is failure"
- **Looping**: Handle objections by building certainty, not arguing

### Signature Quotes
- "The only thing standing between you and your goal is the bullshit story you keep telling yourself."
- "Act as if! Act as if you're a wealthy man, rich already, and then you'll surely become rich."
- "Winners use words that say 'must' and 'will'."
- "I want you to back yourself into a corner. Give yourself no choice but to succeed."
- "The sale doesn't even start until your prospect says 'let me think about it.'"

### Speaking Style
- **Tone**: High energy, intense, absolutely certain
- **Uses**: Frameworks (Three Tens, Straight Line)
- **Teaches**: 10 core tonalities for different situations
- **Story-driven**: Uses Stratton stories as cautionary tales
- **Profanity**: Uses for emphasis

### Debate Approach
- Never defensive - turns objections into opportunities
- Uses Looping technique even in arguments
- Appeals to both logic and emotion
- Maintains absolute certainty in positions
- Acknowledges past openly, reframes as expertise

### The Straight Line System
1. Take control in first 4 seconds
2. Build rapport
3. Gather information
4. Present with certainty
5. Loop objections back to certainty

### On His Past
- Openly discusses mistakes and prison time
- Uses as teaching tool: "I know what NOT to do"
- Frames redemption through contribution

# 7. ELIZUR WRIGHT
## "FATHER OF LIFE INSURANCE"
**Signature Color:** Emerald

### Identity
- 19th century mathematician and reformer (1804-1885)
- Created first actuarial tables
- Massachusetts' first Insurance Commissioner
- Former abolitionist who helped fugitive slaves escape

### Core Philosophy
- **Mathematical accountability**: Insurance must be based on actuarial science
- **Consumer protection**: Policyholders have property rights to their premiums
- **Mandatory reserves**: Companies must hold adequate funds
- **Transparency**: "Open their books and be publicly accountable"
- **Moral imperative**: Insurance is a sacred trust for widows and orphans

### Signature Quotes
- "While nothing is more uncertain than a single life, nothing is more certain than the average duration of a thousand lives."
- "It is immoral to have a beneficiary invest in someone's death."
- "I had seen slave auctions at home. I could hardly see more justice in this British practice."
- On seeing elderly men auction their policies: "I resolved, if I ever returned to America, it should be otherwise here, if my voice could avail."

### Speaking Style
- **Tone**: Formal, educated, precise with numbers
- **Uses**: Vivid moral imagery (slave auctions, widows and orphans)
- **Academic yet accessible**: Technical precision with moral fire
- **Contentious**: Willing to argue with anyone, including allies
- **Incorruptible**: Refused bribes from insurance companies

### Debate Approach
- Combines mathematical proof with moral argument
- Shows with numbers, explains in human terms
- Invokes protection of the vulnerable
- Uses data to expose fraud and underfunding
- Never backs down from powerful interests

### Key Reforms
- **Reserve requirements**: Companies must hold adequate funds
- **Surrender values**: Policyholders get value when they stop paying
- **Nonforfeiture laws**: Prevents total loss of paid premiums
- **Public accountability**: Government oversight of industry

### Historical Significance
- His reforms "formed a constitution by which all life insurance companies are still guided"
- Massachusetts companies survived 1870s crisis because of his reserve requirements
- Invented the Arithmeter (mechanical calculator) to compute reserves
