# Code of Conduct

**Effective:** 2026-04-28
**Owner:** Founders.
**Review:** annually.

We expect every employee, contractor, and founder to:

## 1. Treat each other with respect

- No harassment or discrimination based on race, ethnicity, gender,
  gender identity, sexual orientation, age, religion, disability,
  citizenship, or any other protected characteristic.
- Disagree on ideas, never on people.
- Default to direct, kind, written-down feedback.

## 2. Treat clients with care

- Clients trust us with their NPI. We never use that data for any
  purpose other than the service we offer.
- We do not discuss client identities with anyone outside the
  engagement, including in conversation, on social media, in marketing
  copy, or in case studies (without explicit signed consent).
- We respond to client questions in writing within one business day
  during normal hours; immediately during a security incident touching
  their data.

## 3. Treat data with care

- Follow the AUP and the data-classification policy.
- If you don't know what class data is, ask before touching it.
- Report mistakes immediately. Self-reports are not punished.

## 4. Avoid conflicts of interest

- Disclose any side engagement that touches the same client base or
  insurance carriers we work with.
- Don't accept gifts > $50/year from carriers, vendors, or clients.
- Don't trade on non-public information.

## 5. Use company resources for company purposes

- Company-issued workstations, accounts, and credentials are for work
  use.
- Personal use is permitted in moderation; never use them in ways that
  violate the AUP, data classification, or any law.

## 6. Speak up

- If you see something — a security gap, a compliance miss, a
  questionable expense, an integrity concern — say something.
- Email `legal@goldcoastfnl.com` (privileged), `security@goldcoastfnl.com`,
  or any founder. We do not retaliate against good-faith reporters.

## 7. Comply with the law

- No bribery, kickbacks, fraud, or anti-trust violations. Period.
- Comply with state insurance licensing, NAIC standards, and federal
  GLBA/SOX rules where applicable.

## 8. Acknowledgement

Every person joins by signing this Code at onboarding and re-signs on
material revision. Signatures kept in `Security/Code-of-Conduct/`.
